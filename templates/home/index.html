Do you know what you drink?

If you'd like to know the difference between juice, nectar and a fruity drink keep reading :)

In accordance with the law, from October 28, 2013 (Directive 2012/12 / EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of April 19, 2012 relating to fruit juices and the Regulation of the Minister and Rural Development of February 8, 2013 - Journal of Laws of 2013, item 327) it is forbidden to add any sugars (e.g. glucose, fructose, sucrose (white sugar), glucose-fructose syrup) to all fruit and tomato juices placed on the market (in a bottle and cartons), NFC and reconstituted from concentrated, pasteurized and unpasteurized juices ) or other sweeteners. These juices contain exactly the same type of sugar and in the same amount as the sugar contained in the fruit or tomatoes from which this juice was produced.

Juices can be divided according to various criteria, taking into account: the type of raw material (fruit, vegetable, fruit and vegetable), type of semi-finished product (directly squeezed, reconstituted from concentrated juice), thermal treatment (unpasteurized - so-called one-day, pasteurized), appearance and consistency (clear, naturally cloudy, pur√©y).

Juice


100% FRUIT INTAKE


Juice is squeezed directly from the fruit or obtained from concentrated fruit juice by recreating the proportion of water and aroma removed during the thickening process.
A juice does not contain any artificial colors or flavors.
It cannot be chemically preserved.

Nectar


FROM 25 TO 99% OF THE FRUIT INPUT


Diluted juice with water with added sugar, possibly citric acid. A nectar does not contain artificial colors or flavors and it cannot be chemically preserved.

Fruity Drink


FROM 20 TO 99% OF THE FRUIT INPUT


Contains a certain amount of juice, water, sugar, acid lemons and other permitted food additives. A fruity drink may contain dyes and aromas, as well as, others additives.
